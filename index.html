Is BMI Still Relevant?

The Body Mass Index (BMI) remains one of the most widely used screening tools for weight-related health risks. By analyzing the ratio of your weight to your height, medical professionals can estimate if you are at risk for conditions like hypertension or diabetes.

Important: Ethnicity Matters

Standard WHO cut-offs are based on European data. Recent studies show that South Asian and East Asian populations have higher body fat percentages at lower BMIs, meaning metabolic risks start at a BMI of 23 instead of 25.

Common Categories

  • Underweight (< 18.5): Potential nutrient deficiencies or osteoporosis risk.
  • Normal (18.5 - 24.9): Statistically the lowest risk for heart disease.
  • Overweight (25.0 - 29.9): Increased strain on joints and the cardiovascular system.
  • Obese (30.0+): High correlation with metabolic syndrome and sleep apnea.

The Mechanics of Your Loan

An Equated Monthly Installment (EMI) consists of two parts: the Principal (the actual money borrowed) and the Interest (the bank's profit). In the beginning, most of your payment goes toward interest, while in the later years, more goes toward the principal.

Why Tenure Matters

Choosing a longer tenure (e.g., 20 years instead of 10) will lower your monthly payment, but it will significantly increase the total amount of interest you pay over the life of the loan. This tool helps you find the "sweet spot" between monthly affordability and total debt cost.

Pro Tip: Using an EMI calculator before taking a loan helps you negotiate better rates with banks and prevents over-leveraging your finances.

Ideal Body Weight (IBW)

What Defines "Ideal"?

The Ideal Body Weight (IBW) calculation isn't about "looks." It is a medical metric used to determine dosage for certain medications and to set healthy baseline targets for patients recovering from surgery or managing chronic illness.

We use the Devine Formula, which is the most widely accepted standard in clinical settings. However, it's important to remember that this doesn't account for bone density or muscle mass. A professional athlete will almost always weigh more than their "ideal" IBW due to dense muscle tissue.

Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR)

Your Body's Engine

Think of BMR as the "idle speed" of a car. It is the number of calories your body burns just to stay alive—breathing, circulating blood, and maintaining internal organs—without any additional movement.

Factors Affecting BMR:

  • Age: BMR usually decreases as you age.
  • Muscle Mass: Muscle burns more calories at rest than fat.
  • Hormones: Thyroid issues can significantly speed up or slow down your rate.

TDEE (Daily Calorie Burn)

The Key to Weight Control

Your Total Daily Energy Expenditure (TDEE) is the actual number of calories you burn daily including your movement. This is the most accurate number to use when planning a diet.

How to use this number:

  • To Lose Weight: Consume 300-500 calories less than your TDEE.
  • To Gain Weight: Consume 300-500 calories more than your TDEE.
  • To Maintain: Eat exactly your TDEE amount.
